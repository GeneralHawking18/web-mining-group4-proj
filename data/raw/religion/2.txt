RichTextJSON-root"><p class="_1qeIAgB0cPwnLhDF9XSiJM">Definition:</p><p class="_1qeIAgB0cPwnLhDF9XSiJM">-- Pantheism: the belief that the universe itself is the divine.</p><p class="_1qeIAgB0cPwnLhDF9XSiJM">-- "∈" : means "is a member of" (e.g 5 ∈ The set of all prime numbers).</p><p class="_1qeIAgB0cPwnLhDF9XSiJM">For completeness, the argument from contingency goes as follows:</p><ol class="_1eJr7K139jnMstd4HajqYP"><li class="_3gqTEjt4x9UIIpWiro7YXz"><p class="_1qeIAgB0cPwnLhDF9XSiJM">Everything is either contingent or non-contingent.</p></li><li class="_3gqTEjt4x9UIIpWiro7YXz"><p class="_1qeIAgB0cPwnLhDF9XSiJM">If everything was contingent, there'd be an infinite regress of contingency (absurd).</p></li><li class="_3gqTEjt4x9UIIpWiro7YXz"><p class="_1qeIAgB0cPwnLhDF9XSiJM">Thus, there must be something that is non-contingent.</p></li></ol><p class="_1qeIAgB0cPwnLhDF9XSiJM"><strong class="_12FoOEddL7j_RgMQN0SNeU">Thesis: The argument of contingency points to Pantheism.</strong></p><p class="_1qeIAgB0cPwnLhDF9XSiJM">The argument goes as follows:</p><ol class="_1eJr7K139jnMstd4HajqYP"><li class="_3gqTEjt4x9UIIpWiro7YXz"><p class="_1qeIAgB0cPwnLhDF9XSiJM">Let A be the set of all contingent things (e.g. our universe, potentially multiverses and whatnot).</p></li><li class="_3gqTEjt4x9UIIpWiro7YXz"><p class="_1qeIAgB0cPwnLhDF9XSiJM">If A is contingent, then A ∈ A -----(since A would contain anything contingent, including itself).</p></li><li class="_3gqTEjt4x9UIIpWiro7YXz"><p class="_1qeIAgB0cPwnLhDF9XSiJM">If <em class="_7s4syPYtk5hfUIjySXcRE">A</em> ∈ <strong class="_12FoOEddL7j_RgMQN0SNeU">A</strong>, then <strong class="_12FoOEddL7j_RgMQN0SNeU">A</strong> is contingent on <em class="_7s4syPYtk5hfUIjySXcRE">A</em> ------(the same way an atom is contingent on its subatomic constituents).</p></li><li class="_3gqTEjt4x9UIIpWiro7YXz"><p class="_1qeIAgB0cPwnLhDF9XSiJM">We can chain the relation A ∈ A to get an infinite descending sequence: ... ∈ A ∈ A ∈ A.</p></li><li class="_3gqTEjt4x9UIIpWiro7YXz"><p class="_1qeIAgB0cPwnLhDF9XSiJM">Parsing the infinite sequence of (4) in the language of (3) gives: A is contingent on A, which is contingent on A, which is again contingent on A, ... ad infinitum.</p></li><li class="_3gqTEjt4x9UIIpWiro7YXz"><p class="_1qeIAgB0cPwnLhDF9XSiJM">This is an infinite regress of contingency hence absurd.</p></li><li class="_3gqTEjt4x9UIIpWiro7YXz"><p class="_1qeIAgB0cPwnLhDF9XSiJM">Since A being contingent leads to absurdity, A must be non-contingent.</p></li></ol><p class="_1qeIAgB0cPwnLhDF9XSiJM">Recall that A is tentatively the set that would contain our universe (and potentially other universes etc). Now</p><ol class="_1eJr7K139jnMstd4HajqYP"><li class="_3gqTEjt4x9UIIpWiro7YXz"><p class="_1qeIAgB0cPwnLhDF9XSiJM">There could <em class="_7s4syPYtk5hfUIjySXcRE"><strong class="_12FoOEddL7j_RgMQN0SNeU">only be one non-contingent thing</strong></em> (which is given the title "God")</p></li><li class="_3gqTEjt4x9UIIpWiro7YXz"><p class="_1qeIAgB0cPwnLhDF9XSiJM">But A and God are both non-contingent</p></li><li class="_3gqTEjt4x9UIIpWiro7YXz"><p class="_1qeIAgB0cPwnLhDF9XSiJM">They must be the same thing.</p></li></ol><p class="_1qeIAgB0cPwnLhDF9XSiJM">Thus, God is simply a label given to the set of all contingent things like our universe and so on, i.e. Pantheism.</p><p class="_1qeIAgB0cPwnLhDF9XSiJM">Potential objection:</p><ol class="_1eJr7K139jnMstd4HajqYP"><li class="_3gqTEjt4x9UIIpWiro7YXz"><p class="_1qeIAgB0cPwnLhDF9XSiJM">"The language of contingency doesn't apply to <em class="_7s4syPYtk5hfUIjySXcRE">sets</em>" ----- A set is just a label we give to a particular group of things.
